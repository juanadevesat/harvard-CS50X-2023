Bartending and soft skills

I worked in the bartending industry for close to eight years, and it was an amazing experience. However, like every job, it had its ups and downs.

I enjoyed working as a bartender because it allowed me to meet different people from different walks of life. I loved hearing their stories and sharing a laugh or two with them. I also enjoyed the creativity that came with bartending. I felt an incredible sense of accomplishment when I created a new cocktail that my customers loved.

However, the downside to bartending was the late hours. I found myself working at night and waking up late in the morning. I barely had any time to myself, let alone a personal life. I found myself feeling tired and overworked, and eventually, I experienced burnout.

After much reflection, I decided to leave the bartending industry and pursue a different career. It was a tough decision to make, but I know it's the best one for me. I don't want to wake up one day and realize that I had spent my entire life working while missing out on everything else life has to offer.

As an experienced bartender, I have a wide range of soft skills that can benefit a computer programmer in various ways. Firstly, bartenders are experts in communication, which involves actively listening and speaking effectively with clients. They can offer personalized customer service and resolve conflicts and complaints calmly and professionally. These skills are essential for a computer programmer, who often works on a team and communicates with clients and other stakeholders.

Additionally, bartenders have excellent multitasking abilities, they can juggle multiple tasks and prioritize their workflow to ensure everything runs smoothly. They can switch between tasks with little notice and handle unexpected problems efficiently. These skills can translate well into a programming project, where a developer can face many complex issues that could jeopardize the project timeline.

Furthermore, bartenders have exceptional attention to detail, which is essential for ensuring that the drinks they make meet the customers' expectations. Programming also requires attention to detail as developers have to write clean, concise, and error-free code that complies with the project requirements.

Finally, bartenders have the ability to remain calm under pressure. They work in a fast-paced and sometimes stressful environment, but they handle every situation with a cool head. A computer programmer can face the same pressure as they work on complex projects with tight deadlines, but those who master these skills can complete their work without letting the pressure get to them.
